Custom Built - FliteTest Bloody Wonder FPV

After building the awesome little FliteTest Bloody Wonder, I wanted to build something that could go farther and higher with less power. So I figured more wingspan and a bigger motor/prop.

It’s pretty straight forward – just double the length of the wing and lengthen the fuselage a bit. It resulted in this for me:

My maiden flight resulted in the plane shooting almost straight up… LOTS of lift. I later determined that I put a motor that was probably way too big for it, having done calculations for only half of the full wingspan using WebOCalc.

My second flight with my GoPro camera resulted in me being overly ambitious to get some neat video and I lost my signal at about 0.42 miles away. (First flight here) I spent almost 2 hours looking for the plane, as it had flown much farther than I had thought.

At this point I received my FPV gear, and was super eager to try it out. Being super excited… I cut out a quick, temporary mount for the GoPro and got my FPV gear attached.

By my third flight, again, being too ambitious resulted in losing my signal again at about 0.24 miles out. (Pretty disappointed with the range; but I wasn’t using a full range receiver).

After searching for 1.5 hours that evening, and then returning the next day with a step ladder… I eventually found it after another hour of searching. (It was again NOT where I thought it was)

The crash didn’t bode well. But none of the electronics were damaged. Only the battery was lost because the voltage had gone too low being plugged in to the ESC over night.
